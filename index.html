BLOG

This section contains the blog written by our members and volunteers sharing their experience working with YISC or leading a project with us.

I heard from my friends that the front-end competition was going to happen at our college. As I was little known to front-end I decided to participate. The first day a workshop about front-end session which was very fruitful. That workshop made me understand front-end more clearly and I become confident to participate in the competition for the next day. I presented my front-end as a layout of "ChiyaPasal" - where ideas of youths meet. And to my surprise, I was announced second and selected for the next round. I prepared myself for the next level but somewhere I was anxious about what I'm going to do in live Hackathon. I was in the condition to quit the competition but the organizing committee assure me that it's not that tough as I thought, When the final arrived I went to live hackathon with the outline of the website on my head. But the presence of mentors made me more confidence and I gave my best. I presented and all I could do was wait for the result. The next day the finals were held and I was announced as the winner. My hard work paid off. Code for Change made me close to Youth in Social Change. As per their promise I was offered an internship. Code for Change was a life-changing event for me. I would like to present my gratitude form the bottom of my heart to Youth in Social Change for this opportunity. I was in need of direction and YISC guide me through this.

Sagar Budhathoki Winner of Code for Change I

“A life dedicated to serving others is the life we should all aspire to live.” As a member of YISC, I work towards serving the society and making an impact on the society. I believe wisdom comes from experience and the wisdom that one gets from service is truly wisdom worth having. I have studied in Trinity International College and have participated in various social events. I have been able to feel the joy of serving others. Here in YISC, I find it easier to take steps towards making a better society with an amazing team. I have been fortunate to be a part of this movement.

Siddhartha Neupane Member

The phrase "Male and female are the two wheels of a cart" is always spelled out in the class and the mass but never felt the situation by me in my existing society. Still, I myself felt contrite to say that there's is the house where a woman i.e. a mother had to give birth to a large number of the mother (i.e. the daughter) throughout her reproductive lifespan to have a boy baby in her family even in my hometown Pokhara. The present context where we had heard "a grand daughter of age 7 years raped by her grand father", "a girl dying because of Chaupadi Pratha" , "Daughter in law being victimised from mother in law who is also a women" etc. are so gut-wrenching which are the impetus for making me to be the part of Youth In Social Change. After being a part of Youth In Social Change, I have been able to explore me and also fulfill my dream for change in society in different way like ; Recently, I have been able to conduct a "Self Defense Training" programme for the students of Maharajgunj Nursing Campus and Ayurveda Campus with the objective of empowerment of women by increase of confidence, self-reliance and reduced fear of violence with the development of new skills. Youth In Social Change is a great platform for those who wants to bring a change in society through youth (male and female) empowerment , reduced the existing voilence to women and child, utilize the leisure time of youth in science and technology , arts, education, environment, etc to make their times fruitful and work in unity for nation development. Finally, I wanna adjure to every youth to rise together to bring change in society for our own nation development.

Rajina Ghimire Secretary

About Us

Youth in Social Change (YISC) was founded in 2073 by Nirajan Chhetri. A young boy in his teens with the vision of bringing youths together for a social cause. YISC is a youth movement for social upliftment.YISC is a registered non-profit organization.
